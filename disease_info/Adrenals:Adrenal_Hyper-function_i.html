Adrenal hyperfunction (Cushing syndrome) causes adrenal tissue to secrete excess cortisol and is manifested by a constellation of symptoms. Accordingly, ACTH levels are low because the high cortisol levels feed back on the anterior pituitary and inhibit ACTH secretion, so proper adrenal functioning is hindered.

Symptoms depend on which hormones are being over produced:

Androgenic steroids-
Testosterone related hormones: Excessive facial hair, baldness, acne, deeper voice, prominent muscle mass

Cortisol-
Cushing syndrome associated with fragile skin, bruising easy, purple stretch marks, Upper body obestity, Fat around neck, then arms and legs

Aldosterone- Excess aldosterone can cause high blood pressure, low levels of potassium, weakness, muscle aches, spasms, Paralysis.

Epinephrine/norepinephrine:
Hormones from the adrenal medulla which can cause headaches and anxiety symptoms.
