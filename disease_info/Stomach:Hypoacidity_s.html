1. Aloe vera- anti-inflammatory agent. Soothes intestinal lining and helps pass waste due to constipation. Helpful for large and small intestine.

* Can be taken internally and topically. For digestive discomfort, drink the aloe bitters 1-2 tblspns (inner fillet of aloe leaf) 2x daily without food. For constipation- up to 4 tblspns daily.

2. L-glutamine-helpful in large intestine, helps maintain secretory IgA, which functions primarily by preventing the attachment of bacteria to mucosal cells.

* Take 2-5g daily, separated throughout the day. Take on an empty stomach. (at least 2 hours before or after meals)

3. Zinc-carnosine- Active against gastric ulceration, anti-gastritis and mucosal protective activities.

* 75mg 1x daily

4. Raw ACV- (Increases Hcl and pepsin)

* 2 tblspns 2x daily- Best taken 30 minutes before meals.

5. Ginger- Calms inflammation from esophageal acid reflux

* 2-4 grams of Ginger root. Take on empty stomach. No more than 6g should be consumed daily.

6. Probiotic blend: Lactobacillus casei, plantarum, rueter, and salivarius; Bifidobacterium Bifidum, longum- Builds “good” bacteria and destroys bacterial infections within the gut that lead to gastric insufficiency.

* Strains and strengths vary. Start at 70 billion strength. Be sure to add prebiotic foods as well.

7. Digestive enzymes – Proteolytic enzymes help the break down process of chyme, to aid in the process of proper digestion of foods; protease, lipase, bromelain, sucrase, amylase, peptidase

* Mixtures of several enzymes and effective dosages vary widely.

8. Betaine HCl/Pepsin- The active and natural byproduct from gastrin release.

* 600 mg taken 30 minutes before each meal.

9. D-limonene- Relaxes LES to stabilize movement of acid.

* 500mg 30 minutes before each meal
